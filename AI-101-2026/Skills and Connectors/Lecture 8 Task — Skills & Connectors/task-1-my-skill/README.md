# LinkedIn Content Generator

Transform your existing technical knowledge into a complete, reusable LinkedIn content package.

---

## Overview

Writing high-quality LinkedIn content consistently is difficult for engineers and technical professionals. Valuable insights often remain buried in design documents, GitHub repositories, technical notes, meeting transcripts, or research papers because converting them into engaging content requires significant time and effort.

This skill automates that transformation while preserving the author's original ideas and technical depth.

Rather than generating generic AI content, it extracts, organizes, and refines user-provided material into professional LinkedIn assets designed to build long-term authority.

---

## Daily-Life Problem It Solves

Instead of manually:

- Reading through technical notes
- Deciding what is worth posting
- Writing a long-form article
- Creating multiple supporting posts
- Choosing hashtags
- Planning a publishing schedule

...this skill performs the entire workflow automatically from a single source of truth.

---

## Features

- Convert raw notes, documents, repositories, or transcripts into polished LinkedIn content
- Generate an evergreen long-form LinkedIn article
- Produce multiple supporting posts derived from the article
- Create additional independent posts from the same source material
- Recommend relevant hashtags and keyword clusters
- Provide content sequencing and publishing suggestions
- Compile everything into a single editable Word (.docx) document

---

## Input

Provide any of the following:

- Raw pasted text
- Technical notes
- Design documents
- Meeting transcripts
- GitHub repositories or files
- Google Drive documents
- Attached documents

The supplied material becomes the single source of truth for all generated content.

---

## Output

Each execution produces a complete LinkedIn content package containing:

1. Long-form LinkedIn Article
2. Supporting Posts derived from the article
3. Independent LinkedIn Posts
4. Hashtags for each content piece
5. Distribution and publishing recommendations
6. A professionally formatted `.docx` document
